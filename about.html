OVERVIEW

Who We Are

The Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus is an active branch of the Nepal Red Cross Society (NRCS) established at Thapathali Campus, Institute of Engineering, Kathmandu. We are one of the most active youth units in Nepal, committed to promoting humanitarian values among engineering students and youth.

We operate under the guidance of the Nepal Red Cross Society and follow the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement's seven Fundamental Principles. Our volunteers are trained professionals who respond to disasters, conduct blood donation camps, provide first aid, and engage in community service.

Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus serves not only the campus community but extends its reach to surrounding neighborhoods, conducting health camps, disaster preparedness training, and environmental activities throughout the year.

PURPOSE

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To inspire, encourage and initiate at all times all forms of humanitarian activities by the youth so as to prevent and alleviate human suffering, thereby contributing to the maintenance and promotion of human dignity and peace in the world. Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus works to prevent suffering, protect life, and uphold human dignity through trained youth volunteers at Thapathali Campus and the surrounding community.

Our Vision

A world where the power of humanity overcomes the force of violence and where everyone has access to safe blood, emergency care, and disaster relief. We envision a campus and community where every student is a trained volunteer, ready to serve in emergencies. We strive to create a generation of compassionate, skilled youth leaders who embody the Red Cross principles in their daily lives and professional careers.

OUR FOUNDATION

Seven Fundamental Principles

The International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement is guided by seven Fundamental Principles.

Humanity

The Red Cross Movement is born of a desire to bring assistance without discrimination to the wounded on the battlefield.

Impartiality

It makes no discrimination as to nationality, race, religious beliefs, class or political opinions.

Neutrality

In order to continue to enjoy the confidence of all, the Movement may not take sides in hostilities or engage in controversies.

Independence

The Movement is independent. The National Societies must always maintain their autonomy.

Voluntary Service

It is a voluntary relief movement not prompted in any manner by desire for gain.

Unity

There can be only one Red Cross or one Red Crescent Society in any one country. It must be open to all.

Universality

The International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement is worldwide, in which all Societies have equal status.

OUR JOURNEY

History & Milestones

A proud history of service, compassion, and impact.

2005

Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus established at Thapathali Campus as an official branch of Nepal Red Cross Society.

2008

First major blood donation camp organized. Over 100 units collected in a single day.

2011

Disaster response training program launched. First batch of 50 trained volunteers deployed.

2015

Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus played a crucial role in earthquake relief after the devastating 7.8 magnitude earthquake. Volunteers provided first aid and relief to thousands.

2018

Reached milestone of 1,000 registered volunteers. Expanded health programs to surrounding communities.

2020

COVID-19 response – Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus volunteers distributed PPE, conducted awareness campaigns, and supported health workers.

2025

Youth RedCross Circle, Thapathali Campus celebrates 20 years of service with 1,200+ volunteers, 5,000+ blood units collected, and 300+ relief operations completed.
